Introduction

What is SurvivalGAN?

SurvivalGAN is a generative model that produces synthetic survival datasets while preserving the joint distribution of covariates, survival times, and censoring indicators. The point is to let researchers share realistic data without crossing the privacy line.

Why a vanilla GAN isn't enough

A plain GAN treats the data as a generic table. It has no notion of survival time, censoring, or how covariates drive outcomes. The synthetic data ends up with optimistic survival times, censoring flags that don't match the times, and broken covariate to outcome dependencies. That's why a survival aware design is needed.

The MSK-50K dataset

Raw MSK-IMPACT cohort: 54,331 samples from 48,179 patients across 64 cancer types. After cleaning we keep 17 covariates from the 45 raw columns: ten continuous variables (Age, MSI Score, etc.) and seven categorical variables (Sex, Disease Status, etc.).

CTGAN Baseline

Conditional Tabular GAN (Xu et al., 2019). Trained on the {{ ctganDataset }} split to generate synthetic patient rows.

Model Architecture

CTGAN normalises continuous columns by mode and uses a conditional vector built by sampling rows during training for the discrete ones. The generator outputs rows with both column types. The discriminator uses PacGAN, grouping {{ ctganPac }} rows at a time, and trains with Wasserstein loss plus gradient penalty.

Conditional Survival GAN

Survival aware tabular GAN trained on {{ survivalGanDataset }}. Adds conditional generation and a separate time to event model on top of the usual GAN setup.

Model Architecture

Architecture lives in survGAN.py. Conditional tabular GAN with an MLP generator and discriminator. Wasserstein loss with gradient penalty, plus a survival-focused training pipeline.

Conclusion

Side by side metric comparison across all three models on the same evaluation suite.

  • (a) Covariate similarity. How close the synthetic features are to the real ones. Lower is better.
  • (b) Survival distribution similarity. How close the synthetic survival times are to the real ones. Lower is better.
  • (c) Downstream survival utility, measured by Harrell's C on the real test set. Higher is better.
